What makes up a commit

A commit's identity (it's SHA-1 digest) is intimately tied to its entire ancentry.

Analogies and conceptual models

  • "branch" is to "git" as...
    1. "copy" is to "file"
    2. "pointer" is to "C/C++"
    3. "intersection" is to "road"
  • "checkout branch" is to "git" as...
    1. "cd directory" is to "the file system"
    2. "chmod g-rwx,o-rwx file" is to "access permissions"
    3. "restore from last Friday" is to "data backups"

Stashing

  • Use stash instead of add and commit to defer commiting work-in-progress.
  • Think of stash as stack of local, disposable commits.
  • stash operates on tracked files only.

Patching/interactive adding

  • Like add but for control freaks.
  • Allows sub-file adding, patch splitting, and diff editing.
